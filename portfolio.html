About Me

From coordinating communities to securing systems

John Kagori Mwangi is a Regional Programme Coordinator and aspiring cybersecurity professional based in Kenya. He currently manages youth development programmes across 50+ communities and 7 counties in Kenya's North East Region — work that has given him a front-row seat to the challenges of fragmented systems and the power of connected, trustworthy digital infrastructure.

Driven by the conviction that Regional Integration requires digital trust, John is studying cybersecurity with a focus on ethical hacking, penetration testing, and AI-powered security solutions. He is a graduate of the ALX AiCE Professional Foundations programme, where he led the Border Pass X (eCUSTOMS ONE) team project addressing cross-border trade delays at the Busia border, conducted climate-smart agriculture research for smallholder farmers, and independently built an AI agent — demonstrating a commitment to self-directed learning at the intersection of technology and community impact.

What sets John apart is his perspective: he comes from the community side first. He understands how people actually use technology — where they trust it, where they don't, and what makes a system work in real life, not just in theory. He's now building the technical skills (ethical hacking, AI integration, security analysis) to pair with that understanding.

John is currently working towards his CompTIA Security+ certification, practising hands-on through TryHackMe and Hack The Box, and documenting his journey through his blog, CyberMind Journal. His long-term goal is to become an ethical hacker and cybersecurity consultant, securing the digital infrastructure that connects African communities.
